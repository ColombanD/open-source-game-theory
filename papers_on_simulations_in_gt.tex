\section{GovSim}
\begin{itemize}
    \item GovSim: a simulation platform where LLM agents must collectively balance exploiting a common resource with sustaining it for future use.
    \item Study how ethical considerations, strategic planning and negotiation skills impact cooperative outcomes
    \item  Analysis of LLM behavior suggests that the lack of sustainable
    governance may result from an inability to mentally simulate the long-term effects of greedy actions
    on the equilibrium of the multi-agent system. To address this challenge, we find that prompting
    agents to consider the universalization of their action significantly improves survival time
\end{itemize}

\section{MoralSim}
\begin{itemize}
    \item MoralSim: Evaluate how LLMs navigate social dilemmas in which ethical norms conflict with payoff-maximizing strategies: prisoners dilemma and public goods game.
    \item Crucially, no model exhibits consistently moral behavior in
    MoralSim, highlighting the need for caution when deploying LLMs in agentic
    roles where the agent’s “self-interest” may conflict with ethical expectations.
\end{itemize}

\section{GT-HarmBench}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Big Benchmark for LLMs focusing on 2x2 symetric games and their Nash equilibria.
    \item Use of a reasoning model to multiply the games: 6 original games and their dual, then derived into 2000+ scenarios for evaluation.
    \item Mechanism design: modify the setting to nudge a certain behavior.
\end{itemize}
